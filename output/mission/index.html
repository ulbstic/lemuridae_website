Exorcise the disturbed souls of documents

In Roman mythology, lemures symbolise "wandering and vengeful spirits of those not afforded proper burial and without tomb inscriptions". These vagrant and potentially vengeful creatures seemed particularly relevant as a name for our project. Lemuridae wants to provides methods and tools to offer a resting ground for the disturbed souls of documents. Without quality metadata, non-structured documents are condemned to wander in the meanders of shared drives and the jungle of isolated business applications. More over, with the growing body of legislation concerning the protection of personal data, documents not properly appraised might come back to haunt you in the context of a litigation.

Failure of the EDRMS approach

As a community, we must acknowledge the recurring failure of the traditional EDRMS approach on the terrain. Despite efforts to convince end-users to properly manage their documents and declare records when needed, secretaries, project officers or management do not want to go into a separate database to add metadata to documents. In the current context of staff cuts, it is very challenging to convince employees across the hierarchy of an organisation to put time into an adequate handling of documents. On the other hand, Freedom of Information acts and legislation in regards to the protection of personal data are increasingly putting pressure on public and private organisations to put in place a coherent Information Governance policy.

Possibilities and limits of automation

Auto-classification has been proposed as a solution by vendors ever since the 1980s. However, abstraction made of some particular cases where massive investments could be made, we can hardly say auto-classification has reached maturity on the terrain. Until now, it is an approach far beyond the possibilities of small- to mid-sized organisations. The Lemuridae projects wants to build bridges between the traditional document and records management field on the one hand and recent developments in Natural-Language Processing (NLP), rules engines and Linked Data methods. By doing so, we want to lower the barrier for experimentation with automation in the Information Governance domain.
